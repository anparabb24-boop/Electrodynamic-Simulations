# Electrodynamics: Radiation Field Visualization
A Python-based simulation using **Matplotlib** to visualize the radiation component of the electric field generated by an accelerating point charge.

##  Physics & Methodology

### 1. The Radiation Field Equation
Unlike static fields, the **Radiation Field ($\vec{E}_{\text{rad}}$)** depends on the acceleration of the charge. This simulation implements the following equation:

$$\vec{E}_{\text{rad}}(\vec{r}, t) = \frac{-q}{4\pi\epsilon_0 c^2} \frac{1}{\|\vec{r}\|} \vec{a}_{\perp}(t - \|\vec{r}\|/c)$$

Where:
* **$\vec{a}_{\perp}$**: The component of the charge's acceleration perpendicular to the displacement vector $\vec{r}$, evaluated at the **retarded time**.
* **$t - \|\vec{r}\|/c$**: The retarded time, accounting for the finite speed of light ($c$).
* **$\|\vec{r}\|$**: The distance from the charge to the observation point.

This formula demonstrates that radiation is a "far-field" effect, as it only drops off by $1/r$ (inverse distance) rather than $1/r^2$.

##  Kinematics of the Charge

The simulation calculates the field based on the instantaneous acceleration of the charge following these paths:

### A. Circular Motion (Rotation in XY Plane)
For a charge orbiting at radius $R$:
$$x = R \cdot \cos(\text{freq} \cdot \text{frame})$$
$$y = R \cdot \sin(\text{freq} \cdot \text{frame})$$
$$\vec{a} = -\omega^2 \vec{r} \text{ (Centripetal Acceleration)}$$

### B. Linear Oscillation (Z-Axis)
For a charge oscillating along the Z-axis:
$$x = 0, y = 0$$
$$z = R \cdot \sin(\text{freq} \cdot \text{frame})$$
$$\vec{a} = -\omega^2 R \sin(\omega t) \hat{k}$$
