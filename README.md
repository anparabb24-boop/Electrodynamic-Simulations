# Electrodynamics: Retarded Potential Visualization
A Python-based simulation using **Matplotlib** to visualize the electric potential field generated by a moving point charge.

##  Physics & Methodology

### 1. The Potential Formula
In this simulation, we visualize the **Scalar Potential ($V$)**. The field vectors are calculated based on:

$$V = \frac{kq}{r}$$

Where:
* **$k$**: Coulomb's constant ($9 \times 10^9$)
* **$q$**: Magnitude of the charge
* **$r$**: The distance between the observer (grid point) and the source (charge)

### 2. Time Delay (Retarded Potentials)
The simulation accounts for the fact that electromagnetic influences do not travel instantaneously. Information travels at the speed of light ($c$). 

The field you see at a distance $r$ at time $t$ is actually determined by where the charge was at an earlier time, known as the **Retarded Time** ($t_{ret}$):

$$t_{ret} = t - \frac{r}{c}$$

This creates the "spiral" or "ripple" effect seen in the animation, as the field lines must "catch up" to the charge's current position.

##  Kinematics of the Charge

The motion of the point charge is defined by parametric equations. Depending on the simulation mode, the charge follows these paths:

### A. Circular Motion (Rotation in XY Plane)
To simulate a rotating charge (Circular Polarization):

$$x = R \cdot \cos(\text{freq} \cdot \text{frame})$$
$$y = R \cdot \sin(\text{freq} \cdot \text{frame})$$
$$z = 0$$

### B. Linear Oscillation (Z-Axis)
To simulate an oscillating dipole:

$$x = 0$$
$$y = 0$$
$$z = R \cdot \sin(\text{freq} \cdot \text{frame})$$

*Where **freq** represents the angular frequency and **R** is the amplitude/radius.*
